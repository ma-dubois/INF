TitreCours: Capital Structure and Financing Decisions
DescriptionCours: This course will cover long term financial instruments and how they can help the firm to obtain a financing that is consistent with its needs and the markets' condition. Topics that will be covered include the financing's role in the process of value creation for shareholders, capital structure and firm characteristics, the role of the investment banker and regulation, and the evaluation of new offerings, either in the form of bonds or commonshares. We will also cover leasing, credit rating, and venture capital.  &nbsp;  Once completed, this course will enable the student to:   understand the main issues of long term financing decisions;  estimate the cost of financing using debt or equity;  understand the practical aspects of bond financing: prospectus, covenants, and credit rating;  evaluate a lease;  understand the practical aspects of equity financing.
